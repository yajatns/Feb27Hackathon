One AI orchestrator that runs your entire back office.

Here's how it works. A CEO says: hire a Senior Engineer at 190 thousand dollars in San Francisco. That's it. One sentence.

The orchestrator — an autonomous AI — decides what to do. It delegates to five specialist agents, each powered by their own LLM with real tools.

Maya, the HR agent, queries Senso — our policy knowledge base — for salary bands and onboarding requirements. She finds the company band is 170 to 210 thousand.

Sam, the Finance agent, pulls real-time market data from Tavily. He checks salary dot com, levels dot fyi, and ZipRecruiter. The market median is 185 thousand. Sam says: approved, 190K is competitive.

The Compliance agent checks San Francisco labor laws and internal policies. All clear.

Alex, the IT agent, provisions accounts through Yutori — automating portals that have no API.

And Aria connects to any system via Airbyte — 600 plus connectors. Notion, Salesforce, Stripe, Jira.

But here's where it gets interesting. Watch what happens with a lowball offer.

Engineering Director. 120 thousand dollars. In San Francisco.

Sam immediately flags it: Critical red flag. 120K is 41 percent below the market median of 204 thousand.

The Compliance agent raises discrimination concerns.

And the orchestrator? It refuses to proceed. It blocks the hire until the salary is adjusted.

That's autonomous decision-making. Not scripted. Not hardcoded. The AI reasons about the data and makes the right call.

And when the CEO overrides a decision — say, bumping a salary to 210K — that correction feeds back through Neo4j into Senso. The agents literally learn from every human decision and get smarter over time.

One AI. Five autonomous agents. Eight real integrations. Zero mocks.
