# Compliance & Onboarding Checklist — Alex SaaS Inc

*Effective Date: January 1, 2026 | Version 3.0 | Policy Owner: General Counsel + VP People*

## 1. I-9 Employment Verification

### 1.1 Timeline Requirements
- **Section 1** (employee): Completed on or before first day of employment.
- **Section 2** (employer): Completed within 3 business days of first day.
- **Reverification:** Before expiration of temporary work authorization.

### 1.2 Acceptable Documents
- **List A** (identity + work authorization): US Passport, Permanent Resident Card, Employment Authorization Document.
- **List B + C combo:** Driver's license (B) + Social Security card (C), or Driver's license (B) + Birth certificate (C).
- HR must examine original documents — no photocopies.

### 1.3 E-Verify
- Alex SaaS Inc participates in E-Verify for all US hires.
- E-Verify case created within 3 business days of hire.
- Tentative Non-Confirmation (TNC): employee has 8 federal business days to resolve.
- Do NOT take adverse action during TNC resolution period.

### 1.4 Remote I-9 Verification
- Authorized Alternative Procedure: Video call verification with authorized representative.
- Physical examination of documents within 3 business days.
- Representative must complete Section 2 in person; can be notary, HR professional, or company-designated agent.

## 2. Background Check

### 2.1 Standard Background Check (All Employees)
- **Criminal history:** 7-year lookback (federal + state + county).
- **Employment verification:** Last 3 employers (title, dates, eligibility for rehire).
- **Education verification:** Highest degree claimed.
- **SSN trace:** Address history validation.
- Processing time: 3–5 business days.

### 2.2 Enhanced Background Check (Specific Roles)
- **Finance / Accounting roles:** Credit check (with written consent per FCRA).
- **Executive roles (VP+):** Extended employment verification (10 years), media search, civil litigation check.
- **Engineering roles with production access:** Additional identity verification.
- **Roles with access to PII / health data:** OIG/GSA exclusion list check.

### 2.3 Adverse Action Process (FCRA Compliance)
1. **Pre-adverse action notice:** Send candidate copy of report + Summary of Rights.
2. **Waiting period:** Minimum 5 business days for candidate to dispute.
3. **Adverse action notice:** If proceeding, send final notice with: reason, CRA contact info, right to dispute.
4. All adverse actions reviewed by Legal before finalization.

### 2.4 State-Specific Background Check Rules
- **California:** Cannot ask about criminal history before conditional offer (Ban the Box). 7-year limit on criminal records. Individualized assessment required.
- **New York:** NYC Fair Chance Act — no criminal history inquiry until after conditional offer. Article 23-A factors analysis required.
- **Texas:** No statewide ban-the-box for private employers. Credit checks permitted with consent.
- **Washington:** No criminal history inquiry on application. Cannot advertise that applicants with criminal records won't be considered.

## 3. Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

### 3.1 Standard Employee NDA
- All employees sign mutual NDA on or before first day.
- Covers: proprietary information, trade secrets, customer data, business plans, source code, financial data.
- Duration: During employment + 2 years post-employment.
- Exceptions: publicly available information, independently developed information, information received from third party without restriction.

### 3.2 Contractor / Vendor NDA
- All contractors, consultants, and vendors with access to company systems must sign NDA before access granted.
- Duration: During engagement + 3 years post-engagement.
- Must include: data return/destruction clause, audit rights, breach notification requirements.

### 3.3 NDA Enforcement
- Legal tracks all active NDAs in Ironclad.
- Exit reminder: departing employees receive NDA reminder with specific obligations listed.
- Suspected breach: report to Legal within 24 hours. Legal initiates investigation within 48 hours.

## 4. Intellectual Property (IP) Assignment

### 4.1 IP Assignment Agreement
- All employees sign IP Assignment Agreement (PIIA) on or before first day.
- Assigns to company: all inventions, works of authorship, designs, discoveries, and improvements made during employment that relate to company business or use company resources.
- Prior inventions: employee must disclose in Exhibit A any pre-existing IP they wish to exclude.

### 4.2 State-Specific IP Rules
- **California (Labor Code §2870):** Cannot assign inventions developed entirely on employee's own time, without company equipment, unrelated to company business.
- **Washington (RCW 49.44.140):** Similar protections to California — employee inventions on own time/equipment not assignable.
- **Illinois (765 ILCS 1060):** Employee inventions on own time/equipment not assignable unless related to employer's business.
- **All other states:** Standard assignment; prior inventions exclusion list in Exhibit A.

### 4.3 Open Source Policy
- Employees may contribute to open-source projects with manager approval.
- Contributions to projects that compete with company products require Legal review.
- No proprietary code may be released as open source without VP Engineering + Legal approval.
- Company-sponsored open-source projects: Apache 2.0 license preferred, MIT acceptable.

## 5. State-Specific Compliance Requirements

### 5.1 California
- **Wage Theft Prevention Act:** Written notice of pay rate, pay day, employer info at hire.
- **Paid Sick Leave:** Minimum 5 days/40 hours per year (Alex SaaS provides 10 days — compliant).
- **Cal/OSHA:** Workplace safety postings; Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP) required.
- **Pay Transparency:** Salary range must be included in all job postings.
- **CCPA Employee Rights:** Privacy notice at collection; right to know, delete, and opt-out.
- **Sexual Harassment Training:** 2 hours for managers, 1 hour for all employees, within 6 months of hire, biennial refresher.
- **Meal/Rest Breaks:** 30-min unpaid meal break for shifts > 5 hours; 10-min paid rest per 4 hours.

### 5.2 New York
- **NYC Pay Transparency Law:** Salary range in all job postings (city applicants).
- **NY HERO Act:** Workplace safety plan for airborne infectious diseases.
- **Paid Family Leave:** 12 weeks at 67% of average weekly wage (state-administered).
- **Sexual Harassment Training:** Annual, interactive, for all employees.
- **Wage Theft Prevention Act (WTPA):** Written wage notice at hire with pay rate, pay frequency, employer details.
- **NYC Automated Employment Decision Tools (AEDT):** Bias audit required before using AI in hiring.

### 5.3 Texas
- **At-Will Employment:** Standard at-will; no state-mandated sick leave or PTO.
- **Workers' Compensation:** Optional in Texas; Alex SaaS opts IN for all TX employees.
- **Payday Law:** Semi-monthly or monthly pay; overtime within 15 days of period end.
- **No State Income Tax:** Simplifies payroll but employees may owe federal estimated taxes.
- **Texas Workforce Commission:** Unemployment insurance registration within 20 days of first TX hire.

### 5.4 Washington
- **Paid Sick Leave:** 1 hour per 40 hours worked (minimum).
- **Paid Family & Medical Leave (PFML):** Up to 12 weeks; employer + employee premium split.
- **Long-Term Care Tax (WA Cares):** 0.58% employee payroll tax; opt-out available with private LTC insurance.
- **Pay Transparency:** Salary range required in job postings (employer 15+ employees).
- **Non-Compete Restrictions:** Only enforceable for employees earning > $120,559.99/year (2026 threshold). Must be disclosed at or before offer.
- **Equal Pay & Opportunities Act:** Wage and career advancement transparency requirements.

## 6. Onboarding Compliance Checklist (Per New Hire)

### Day 1
- [ ] I-9 Section 1 completed
- [ ] IP Assignment Agreement (PIIA) signed
- [ ] NDA signed
- [ ] Employee handbook acknowledgment signed
- [ ] Direct deposit authorization
- [ ] W-4 (federal tax withholding)
- [ ] State tax forms (if applicable)
- [ ] Benefits enrollment initiated (30-day window)
- [ ] Emergency contact form completed
- [ ] Anti-harassment policy acknowledgment
- [ ] Data privacy policy acknowledgment

### Within 3 Business Days
- [ ] I-9 Section 2 completed (document verification)
- [ ] E-Verify case created
- [ ] Background check cleared (or conditional start documented)
- [ ] IT equipment delivered and enrolled in MDM
- [ ] All system access provisioned per role

### Within 30 Days
- [ ] Benefits elections finalized
- [ ] Security awareness training completed
- [ ] Department-specific compliance training completed
- [ ] 30-day check-in with manager documented
- [ ] Confirm all state-specific requirements met

### Within 90 Days
- [ ] 90-day performance review completed
- [ ] Probationary period assessment
- [ ] Any outstanding compliance items resolved
- [ ] Sexual harassment training completed (CA: 6-month window)
